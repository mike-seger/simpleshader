## Thanks

### Shadertoy & Inigo Quilez

[Shadertoy](https://www.shadertoy.com) by Inigo Quilez and Pol Jeremias is the single greatest resource for learning real-time graphics programming. Many of the techniques used in Simple Shader — signed distance functions, ray marching, domain repetition, procedural texturing — were learned from iq's articles, talks, and shader examples. Thank you for making this knowledge freely available.

- [Inigo Quilez — articles](https://iquilezles.org/articles/)
- [Shadertoy](https://www.shadertoy.com)

### The demoscene

The tracker modules used for audio-reactive demos come from the demoscene — a global community of digital artists who create real-time audiovisual works. The tradition of pushing hardware limits to produce art in kilobytes has been a deep source of inspiration.

- [Mod Archive](https://modarchive.org) — the largest collection of tracker music
- [Pouët](https://www.pouet.net) — demoscene community and production archive

### WebGL & browser vendors

Simple Shader runs entirely in the browser with no build step, no npm, and no server-side code. This is only possible because modern browsers ship a remarkably capable graphics stack. Thanks to the teams behind WebGL, Web Audio, and the ES module system.
